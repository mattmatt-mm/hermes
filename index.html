Lesson 3 · tech

Cache locality and why it wins in low-latency systems

  • Spatial locality — Accessing contiguous memory (arrays) beats pointer-chasing (linked lists) because whole cache lines get used.
  • Temporal locality — Recently touched data is likely reused, so keep your working set small enough to stay in cache.
  • Cache hierarchy and lines — L1/L2/L3 differ hugely in latency and size; a cache line (~64 bytes) is the unit moved between them.
  • False sharing — Two threads writing different variables on the same cache line force costly coherency traffic; pad or partition to avoid it.
  • Measuring and prefetching — Hardware prefetchers hide latency, and tools like perf or cachegrind expose misses so you can fix hot paths.
